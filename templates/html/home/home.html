Location

Located within the level one foyer of the newly built Electrical Engineering Building at the University of Sydney, the new footbridge will not only function as a medium to move from point A to point B, but also as an engaging learning tool for structural health monitoring.

Pedestrian Traffic

With pedestrian traffic constantly changing throughout the day, building users and students can effectively determine periods of low and high foot traffic by observing peak deflection values.

Strain Sensors

The advancement of sensor technology has enabled increasingly sensitive values of deflection to be determined. This correspondingly has meant an increase in the level of accuracy in the data obtained.

Fiber Bragg Grating (FBG) Sensors

Fibre Bragg Grating (FBG) sensors measure changes in temperature and strain at discrete locations along a fibre optic cable. The FBGs are determined by generating periodic variations in the refractive index within the centre of an optical fibre. According to Bragg’s law, when a beam of light travels in the FBG sensor, the light from the broadband source passes through the grating at a particular wavelength, the Bragg wavelength is reflected which relates to the grating period. These changes in the wavelength of the reflected light are linearly proportional to changes in temperature and strain along the optical fibre and enables the computation of temperature and strain changes.

Location Of Sensors Within The Footbridge

There are six strain sensor arrays and two temperature sensor arrays. Each strain sensor array contains 16 sensors at 1.0 m spacing. Each temperature sensor array contains 4 sensors at 5.0m spacing. The strain sensor arrays were divided into two sets of three arrays each. These are referred to as set A and set B. Furthermore, there is one set of two temperature sensor arrays, referred to as set C.The position on plan of the fibre optic sensor arrays for strain and temperature can be seen in the adjacent schematic.

Pedestrian Flow and Changes in Sensor Data.

When pedestrian traffic moves across the footbridge, there are various implications that relate to the changes in magnitude of the data received by the strain and temperature sensors. Such changes can result in fluctuations in various time-dependent structural parameters such as curvature, slope and deflection.
